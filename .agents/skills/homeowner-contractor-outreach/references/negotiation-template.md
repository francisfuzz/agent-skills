# Counter-Offer & Negotiation Email Templates

Use these when a homeowner wants to negotiate a quote before accepting.
Keep the tone respectful — contractors remember difficult customers.

---

## Counter-Offer (Price Too High)

**Subject**: Re: Quote for {Trade Type} — {Your Name}

Hi {Name},

Thank you for the quote — I appreciate you getting back to me quickly.

I've received a couple of estimates for this job, and yours came in a bit
higher than the others at {their quote}. I'm not just looking for the cheapest
option — I care more about getting the job done right — but I want to be
transparent with you.

Is there any flexibility on the price, or is there a version of the scope
that would bring the cost down without cutting corners on quality?

Happy to discuss if you have a few minutes.

Thanks,
{Name}

---

## Request for Itemized Breakdown

**Subject**: Re: Quote — Can You Break This Down?

Hi {Name},

Thanks for the quote. Before I make a decision, would you be able to send me
an itemized breakdown — labor vs. parts/materials? It helps me compare
apples to apples across the estimates I've received.

No rush, just want to make sure I'm making an informed choice.

Thanks,
{Name}

---

## Requesting a Match

**Subject**: Re: Quote — Quick Question

Hi {Name},

I have another quote for this job at {lower price}. I'd prefer to work with
you based on your reviews, but wanted to give you a chance to match or get
close before I decide. Is that something you'd be open to?

Either way, I appreciate your time.

{Name}

---

## Accepting a Quote

**Subject**: Re: Quote — Ready to Move Forward

Hi {Name},

I'd like to go ahead with your quote. I'm available:
- {Option 1: date + time window}
- {Option 2: date + time window}

Let me know which works for you and I'll confirm. Please send your address or
any prep instructions if needed.

Looking forward to working with you.

{Name}
{Phone}
{Address}
