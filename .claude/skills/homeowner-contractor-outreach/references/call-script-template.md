# Phone Call Script — By Trade Type

Use when no email is available, or for emergency/same-day situations.
Keep it under 60 seconds. Have the homeowner read through it once before calling.

---

## Universal Opening
> "Hi, my name is {name} and I'm a homeowner in {city}. I found your business
> online and I'm looking for a licensed {trade type} — do you have a moment?"

---

## By Trade Type

**Plumber**
> "I have a {describe issue: leaking pipe / water heater issue / clogged drain /
> no hot water} at my home. It {started today / has been ongoing for X days}.
> Are you available to come out {urgency window}? And roughly what would a
> service call cost?"

**HVAC**
> "My {furnace / AC / heat pump} is {not heating or cooling / making a noise /
> completely out}. It's {brand} and about {age} years old if that helps. Are you
> able to come {urgency window} and what's your service call fee?"

**Electrician**
> "I have an electrical issue — {describe: tripped breakers / outlet not working /
> panel upgrade needed}. I want to make sure I'm working with a licensed
> electrician. Can you confirm your license and give me a sense of pricing
> before coming out?"

**Roofer**
> "I noticed {describe: missing shingles / leak / storm damage} on my roof. I'm
> looking to get 2–3 quotes before deciding. Are you available for an inspection
> this week? Is there a fee for the estimate?"

**General Handyman**
> "I have a few small repairs that need attention — {describe top 1–2 items}.
> Do you do handyman work and if so, what's your hourly rate or how do you
> typically quote jobs like this?"

---

## Closing (all trades)
> "Great. Can I get your email address so I can send you my availability and
> address? And is there anything you need from me before the visit?"

---

## If They Can't Come
> "No problem — do you have a colleague you'd recommend, or should I check back
> with you in a few days?"
